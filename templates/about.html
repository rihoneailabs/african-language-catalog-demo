About Us

Our Mission

The African Languages Catalog is dedicated to celebrating and preserving the rich linguistic diversity of the African continent. Our mission is to provide a comprehensive, accessible resource for anyone interested in learning about the myriad languages spoken across Africa.

We aim to raise awareness about the importance of language diversity, promote language preservation efforts, and foster appreciation for the cultural heritage embedded in each language.

African Linguistic Diversity

Africa is home to an extraordinary linguistic tapestry, with estimates ranging from 1,500 to over 2,000 distinct languages. This represents about 30% of the world's languages, spoken by only about 17% of the global population.

Some key facts about African languages:

  • The continent is home to several major language families, including Niger-Congo, Afroasiatic, Nilo-Saharan, and Khoisan.
  • Some African countries, like Nigeria and Cameroon, are home to hundreds of languages each.
  • Many African languages are endangered, with some having fewer than 1,000 speakers.
  • Africa is also home to several widely spoken languages, some with over 100 million speakers, such as Swahili, Hausa, and Arabic.

About Our Catalog

Our catalog provides information on a wide range of African languages, including:

  • Language names and alternate names
  • Regions and countries where each language is spoken
  • Approximate number of speakers
  • Language families and sub-families
  • Writing systems used
  • Basic phrases and sample audio (where available)

We strive for accuracy and regularly update our information. However, given the complex nature of language classification and the ongoing research in the field, we welcome feedback and contributions from linguists, native speakers, and language enthusiasts.
